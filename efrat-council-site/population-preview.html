אוכלוסיית אפרת לפי קבוצות גיל, 2009–2024

Efrat population by age group, 2009–2024 — based on Israel Central Bureau of Statistics annual figures.

אוכלוסיית אפרת לפי קבוצות גיל (כאחוז מהסך)

מקור: למ"ס · Source: Israel CBS. Buckets map approximately to 5-year CBS bins.

What the percentages show:
  • The 65+ share more than doubled — from 3.8% in 2009 to 10.6% in 2024. The single clearest structural shift in the town.
  • Children under 5 dropped from 12.1% to 9.6% of the population. Even though the absolute number rose, the under-5 share is shrinking.
  • School-age (5–19) held remarkably steady — 34.7% in 2009, 35.6% in 2024. Efrat remains a school-age town.
  • 20–29 (young adults) peaked in the mid-2010s (~17%) and has fallen back to ~11% — fewer young adults staying or returning.
  • 30–49 (parents) share dipped slightly from 22.7% to 22.6% — essentially unchanged.
  • 50–64 share fell from 13.9% to 10.8% — fewer late-career residents, proportionally.
  • The story in one line: Efrat is aging at the top while the youngest share is shrinking. The school-age core stays steady, but the demographic center of gravity is slowly moving older.
